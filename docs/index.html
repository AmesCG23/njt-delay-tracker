Every weekday, we monitor NJ Transit commuter rail alerts and estimate the economic cost of delays using the U.S. Department of Transportation’s Value of Travel Time methodology, adjusted for New Jersey’s median household income. At $24.00 per hour, a delayed train carrying 800 commuters costs employers roughly $320 in lost productivity per minute. These figures are estimates, not official accounting — but the delays are very real.

NJT/Delay Tracker is a project of BetterTrains.org, a nonprofit that doesn't exist yet (but I have the domain just in case). It's an independent public accountability project and not affiliated with New Jersey Transit in any way, shape, or form.

Our estimates are based on ridership estimates obtained through an OPRA request that someone kindly posted to reddit. We ballpark peak ridership on key lines as around 800-1000 (NEC) and 500 (M&E, MoBo). "Cost" of lost time is based on Department of Transportation estimates of the value of time spent in transit and prevailing New Jersey hourly rates, which we assume are higher for commuters than the average resident. Alerts are scraped from Bluesky pages that repost regular NJT delay alerts. We deduplicate to avoid counting the same delay twice, taking the highest reported delay. We are consantly improving our methodology and welcome feedback.
